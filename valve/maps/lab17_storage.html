Description

Mid afternoon.

This is Research Lab #17, buried deep underground on a remote island. When the subterranean river of slime was first discovered, along with its unearthly fauna and mysterious crystalline growths, a nameless branch of the government scrambled to install a research facility to explore its potential for military applications. Soon after construction was completed, the installation suffered structural damage due to the inexorable growth of the crystals embedded in the surrounding rock. In particular, part of the rail tunnel -- connecting the laboratory itself with the warehouse and the outside world -- weakened and collapsed.

This level consists of the entrance section and the warehouse -- and, of course, the river of slime. Be warned, something hungry lives in the slime. Nobody has ever seen it -- nobody still living, that is -- but it can be heard patrolling its domain.

The laboratory itself is also available as Lab17: SlimeLab.

Story

What story? It's DeathMatch: just kill anything that moves, and have fun!

Features

All the weapons can be found in this level, but they are not necessarily easy to pick up -- and the Egon Backpack Gun is rather well hidden... Navigation through the level, and acquisition of many of the 'high-powered' weapons, will require a fair bit of jumping around (remember: crouch-jump is your friend!) or exposure to unfriendly fire. Or both. Several sniping positions exist for those who prefer sneaking to running, but getting to them can be fraught with danger. Watch your step -- and don't go swimming.

The gauss cannon (aka "jump gun") is not available in Lab17: Flood -- but the Egon cannon is a little easier to find.

Both versions of this level have been extensively playtested by the following people, all of whom have made comments contributing to the finished product:

Storage seems to be slightly slow with only two people, but three or four seems to work out pretty well. I suspect it would support as many as eight or ten players before becoming too crowded; we have not, unfortunately, tested it with more than five at a time. As a smaller variant, Flood works fine with two players, and with five or six becomes quickly chaotic.

Comments

After completing LavaLab, the decision to build another lab to study another dangerous fluid seemed almost automatic, and SlimeLab was born.

Revision History

Version 1.4 -- May 19, 2005

As well as adding several new features to Flood, it was necessary to roll some changes back into Storage for the sake of consistency. Most of the changes are cosmetic, although there were a few glitches that we discovered when playing the previous release...

Version 1.3 -- April 5, 2005

After completing SlimeLab (and its counterpart, Quake,) I decided to generate a matching counterpart for Storage. Thus Flood was born, and some of the features in it had to be rolled back into Storage. For a more detailed look at the history of the intertwined development of all four Lab17 levels, see Lab17: History.

Version 1.2 -- September 12, 2004

We have been playing this level for at least three months, and there have been very few complaints. However, there are a couple of minor issues that I felt needed addressing, and a couple of consistency tweaks that have grown out of my work on Lab17: SlimeLab:

  • Made the ceiling flouro-bars a little more streamlined as per Lab17: SlimeLab; this should also resolve the problem of bumping into them whilst jumping down the corridor.
  • Found the HOM error mentioned previously (Version 1.1); added a ledge in an effort to recalculate the surface causing the error. After further testing I realised the error was actually caused by being able to see further than the maximum view distance set in the level, and fixed it by increasing the maximum view distance. The ledge I added remains, though; I rather like it.
  • Added yet another startpoint or two.
  • Added a few more creepers blocking any holes in the rock where the sun is shining in; this was partly in response to having played the level in a mod with a grappling hook which allowed access to those areas which should not normally be accessible -- and which were therefore not designed to be viewed up close...
  • Added a couple more items in out-of-the-way places...
  • Initially added a radiation-detector-clicking effect to the slime river; it worked well, but was a little distracting -- and it appeared to increase the lagginess of the level -- so I scrapped it again.

Version 1.1 -- June 14, 2004

Primarily a bug-fix:

  • Widened the gap between a particular pile of boxes and the wall of the warehouse, so that the player can now fit through the gap as initially intended.
  • Investigated reports of an HOM error, but could not locate it.
  • Attempted to improve the accessibility of one particular climbable surface that seemed to be giving more trouble than it should. Only partial success.
  • The alarm lights around the freight lift looked great, but every time they went off, server latency went out the window. I have reduced the brightness of these lights, and hopefully fixed the problem.

Also made a couple of slight design changes, nothing too radical:

  • It seemed that nobody was using the top sniper spot. I asked a few questions, and the main problem -- apart from the difficulty of actually getting to it -- was the plantlight in the tunnel which nicely silhouetted any sniper foolish enough to sit up there. I removed the plantlight and placed a light crystal opposite the entrance instead: the sniper hole is still visible, but a sniper waiting there should be mostly invisible...
  • Added another ledge along the river, for those adventurous souls who wish to move around outside a little more freely.
  • Expanded the range of the thing in the river. :-)
  • Added an FPC. While I was consulting with one of my playtesters, I asked "Is there anything else that I need to add? Move the light, add a ledge...?" He said "freshly plucked chicken", so of course, without batting an eyelid I added "freshly plucked chicken" to the "To Do" list. It is in the level somewhere -- and it is not happy!
  • Added an extra multiplayer startpoint or two. Or six. Every startpoint should be reasonably close to a reasonable weapon, generally either a shotgun or an AR9mm -- except perhaps one...

Version 1.0 -- May 30, 2004

Initial release.

Known Bugs

  • I really blew the budget with this one. Some areas are just way too complex for the software display driver to handle. All I can recommend is that you run this level under the OpenGL driver, or not at all. I am sorry if this inconveniences anyone, but I really cannot see a fix for it without completely redesigning the level. I promise my next level (um, after SlimeLab) will be a lot more playable for all. The r_speeds seem to peak at around 1550, so these levels may not be playable on a low-end machine. They run just fine on a 2GHz Celeron.
  • There are some strange shadows in Flood which have me baffled; the wall in question appears to be carved up in odd ways. Of course, after some of the problems I've encountered while attempting to compile Flood, I can live with a few strange shadows...
